Student Projects

I have supported various student-led projects over the years at Bath Spa University, including but not limited to the following highlighted projects.

Degree show

year - ongoing


At the end of each academic year, the final year students at Bath Spa University put on an end-of-year show, which represents the culmination of their studies over the past three years. As a technican I support this event, my role is quite varied, and I may be called upon to assist with a variety of tasks, including working within my area of specialist support, as well as doing more general tasks like painting walls and hanging artwork. However, the main focus of my support is typically centered around AV equipment, printing, mounting of prints, and creating projection spaces. By helping to ensure that the necessary equipment and facilities are in place, I aim to facilitate a successful and memorable end-of-year show that truly showcases the hard work and dedication of these talented students.

workshop Support

year - ongoing


As a workshop technican for moving image at Bath Spa University, I am responsible for helping students with a wide range of tasks related to productions, from editing films shot on phones to working with 4K film footage and animation. One of the key aspects of my role is assessing the skill level of each individual student and adapting quickly to their needs, providing demonstrations that are tailored to their particular abilities. By providing this personalized support, I strive to help each student reach their full potential and succeed in their artistic endeavors.

Porthleven Prize

year - ongoing


Every year, Bath Spa University offers the Porthleven prize to twenty students, giving them the opportunity to participate in a five-day residency with workshops. From these twenty students, ten will be selected to undergo a fully-funded twelve-day residency at the Porthleven Lifeboat Art Studio. As a technician for the university's students, I have helped by providing assistance in the form of equipment and setup for their final residency shows. In most cases, this has involved setting up projection equipment, but the extent of my involvement has varied depending on the needs of the prize winners. By helping to create an environment in which these students can showcase their talents, I am proud to contribute to the success of these up-and-coming artists.

Phoebe Wolley

year 2022


Phoebe, a ceramic artist and dancer, explored the decomposition of unfired ceramic pots for her final Master of Art piece. Her work was displayed in the Holburne Museum in Bath, showcasing her unique and innovative approach to ceramic art. In order to support Phoebe's artistic vision, I provided assistance in a number of ways. Firstly, I helped to set up lighting and video equipment in the lighting studios, ensuring that her work was presented in the best possible light. Additionally, I provided support in editing her final piece, helping her to refine and perfect her vision. Finally, I assisted in setting up the projection and sound equipment for her final year show, allowing her to fully showcase her work to a wider audience.

Alyson Minkley

year 2020


Datafield is a collaborative and participatory performance artwork designed for video, created across multiple disciplines. The final cut of the project was shortlisted for the Visions of Science ArtPrize at The Edge in 2020 and long-listed for UK New Artist in 2022. The project has been screened at several venues, including Centre of Gravity in Bristol in 2020 and Wiltshire Creative at Salisbury Playhouse in 2021. Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, I assisted Alyson by providing support in editing the video footage into the final piece. Additionally, I helped with the setup of projection and sound equipment for her final year show, making it possible for her to present her work in the best possible light.

Ben Jones

year 2016


During his time at Bath Spa University, Ben delved into photography as a fine art medium and created a final piece that focused on scale and photos of the moon's surface. To support his studies, I aided in the production of his final year project by producing large prints of his work, which were dry mounted onto aluminum backing boards and hung in a gallery space. By showcasing his work in this manner, Ben was able to fully express his vision and demonstrate the potential of photography as a fine art medium.
