Research Projects

I have supported academic staff in various research projects, and the following are some highlighted projects that I have worked on

Accelerate

year 2021-2023


The Erasmus+ funded 'Strategic Partnership' project, 'ACCELERATE: Accessible Immersive Learning for Art and Design'; coordinated by Bath Spa University and run in collaboration with the five other European Institutions listed below, began in 2021 and will ran for two years.

The project provided opportunity for art and design lecturers, researchers and learning technologists from across the world to reflect on the impact of Covid-19 on higher education teaching, and to explore new possibilities for pedagogy and digital innovation.

Support


My responsibility for this project includes demonstrating VR equipment and software to both staff and students, as well as documenting experiences and events taking place at BSU.

Technology in Education and the Classroom

year 2017-2020


This research project aimed to explore how technology can be used to enhance the educational experience and pedagogy of lessons in the classroom. The project evaluated various user-friendly technologies and assessed their potential benefits and drawbacks, considering factors such as student engagement and learning outcomes. Following the completion of the research, some of the key findings were incorporated into a multi-million-pound development project for a new campus, creating a more modern and efficient learning environment for students and staff.

Support


As part of my responsibilities for this project, I collaborated with the learning technologist to explore various technologies that could enhance classroom learning. This involved setting up and maintaining the equipment.

MediaWall

year 2014-2019


MediaWall, spanning two storeys of the atrium at Bath Spa University, it has successfully hosted more than 50 projects from international artists and researchers over the course of five years. Through these projects, MediaWall has created a space that encourages the process of learning through creation, effectively erasing the boundaries between bodies and space, as well as between movement and dimensionality.

Composed of 30 HD display panels arranged in a 3x10 grid, MediaWall measures 7.35 meters in height and 3.75 meters in width.

Support


Over the years of MediaWall's use at Bath Spa University, I have collaborated with numerous visiting artists, including Larry Cuba, the original animator of Star Wars films, the designers of Monument Valley, and Bruce Munro. My role was to create large visual reproductions of their work in various formats for this impressive video wall.
